The story starts off with the two, once best friends, riding a train. They talk about current events until they start to reminisce about the fateful winter where they stopped being friends. We travel back to the past as the two boys play Pakemon, while subtly saying their problems at home. The two start talking about the statue in the mountain and decide to go on a journey to break it together. After breaking the statue, the blue kid wants to run away with the green kid. He is hesitant for a second but then agrees.

As exploring through the forest, the two come across a dead animal. After inspecting it, the two pass out. A monster comes up to the boys, looking like it’s inspecting them, then walks off. When the boys wake up, that’s when they are introduced to the two different realities, the green kid sees animals and vibrant colors while the blue kid sees robots and machinery while the colors are a bit dull. As they go through the forest, they encounter different characters (A couple looking for their son, a hunter and his dog, and the boy Haru). Each has their own design depending on the different perspectives the kids see. They run into problems and have to make certain decisions based on what they saw. After a while, they discuss how they see their world and its differences. Time passes and they come across a boy who doesn’t look part of the theme, he’s just a boy. They talk to the boy by the campfire and stay the night in the cave.

The two boys reached the top and encounter the statue. Turns out the fireworks they brought couldn’t blow up the statue. After not being able to blow up the statue, the two laugh about the firework and then pass out once again. Once they come to, they see the world as normal. The blue kid changed his mind on running away from home and the green boy just followed him to keep him safe. They suddenly come across the same creature that inspected them when passed out and see it swallow a woman. They have a life or death situation where one has to live and the other dies. The one who left comes back to save their best friend and drive the monster away while saving the woman who was swallowed. We travel back to the train where we started and get the epilogue of what happened upon returning from the mountain. The two drifted apart after some time occurred.

The game is a two player co-op puzzle platformer. The two traverse through the forest as humans at first, then to whatever the other sees. They learn what the dangers are by dying and being able to come back to life. Upon entering the different perspectives route, the two players see different things and are able to interact with certain objects. Communication is important once reaching this point. One player isn’t able to do something while the other player can. There are times when the two can’t talk to each other because of different cutscenes playing out. Depending on the decision the two make, it’ll lead to certain events later in the game. Each perspective has its own noise and music.
